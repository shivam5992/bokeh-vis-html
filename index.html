Glimpse of all Google's Acquisitions


Since its inception in 1998, Google has acquired over 200 companies across different verticals. Deja News was the first company that got acquired in Feburary 2001 and ended up as core component of Google Groups. As of April-2017, Kaggle Inc which is a platform for Artificial Intelligence and Data Science is the most recent addition in Google's Portfolio. A large number of acquisitions were observed in the period 2014-2015 when the company reorganized them and their operations under new entity called Alphabet Inc.


Till date, Motorola remains the costilest purchase of google amounting to $12.5 Billion, while NestLabs and Youtube are other costiler acquisitions.

Google acquired companies under different categories such as Mobile, AI, Cloud, Robotics, Maps, Communication, Search and Publication. AI and Mobile are the categories with most number of acquisitions.

In terms of geographic spread, plethora of acquisitions were made from United States. Canada and Switzerland are ranked two and three respectively.




Acquisitions by Year



About 40% of the total acquisions were observed in 2012-2016 with total 79 acquisitions, No doubt the period was a balloning era of global startups across different sectors. Current time period, ie. 2016-Present is also observing a the high rate of acquisitions, with on an average 1 acquisition per week.





Acquisitions by CEOs



Though Eric Schmid served as the longest CEO for Google (~10 years), Number of acquisitions remained less (33) as compared to the number of acquistions by his successors : Larry Page and Sundar Pichai, (90 and 82+ respectively).





AI and Mobile !!!


With 45 acquisitions under Mobile category, the trend indicates the focus on solidifying Andriod as the boss of all operating system. This set also involes the notorious acquisition of Motorola in 2012 for a whopping $125 Million. AI : Artificial Intelligence is another top category which is rising very fastly, it includes companies focussing on Deep Learning, Machine Learning, NLP, Chatbots, Image Processing and Data Science, indicating the importance of intelligence in each product that Google is making.






Acquisitions by cost
